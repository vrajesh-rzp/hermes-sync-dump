Summary
Ishan Nahata initiated a discussion leading to the decision to focus on design experiments to increase the registration number by 2% in December and postpone smart assist until January to allow for better design and requirements iteration, which Kan agreed with, while Gopi Bhatnagar inquired about the postponement and their upcoming leave. Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Discussion on December Focus and Smart Assist Delay Ishan Nahata initiated a discussion about focusing on subscriptions rather than smart assist for December, aiming to complete a goal. Kan’s point was to choose one of the two projects to avoid starting something and not finishing it, suggesting smart assist would likely come in January, allowing for more time on design iterations and requirements. Gopi Bhatnagar inquired about the decision to postpone smart assist for December and mentioned that they would be on leave for two weeks starting December 15th.
* Design Experiments for Registration Number Improvement The team decided to focus on design experiments to increase the registration number by 2% in December. Ishan Nahata suggested brainstorming five to six pure front-end and design-focused ideas, which could include copy changes, layout changes, or UI/UX tweaks based on psychological principles like font size and contrast. They planned to share these ideas and prioritize the most promising ones this week. Gopi Bhatnagar confirmed that for now, they are not focusing on unreg merchants.
* WhatsApp Integration and Merchant Data Collection Gopi Bhatnagar asked if WhatsApp integration could be included in the design experiments, and Ishan Nahata confirmed it could, detailing that marketing would handle the communications sent through a created event pipeline. Ishan Nahata also suggested collecting data from registered merchants, not just those who did not subscribe or chose a light plan, to gain better insights into their decisions, referencing a conversation where a merchant wasn't aware of the monthly payment structure.
* Proposed Design Solutions and Custom Plan Flow Gopi Bhatnagar suggested a slider and calculator as a potential design element, but Ishan Nahata mentioned it was blocked and suggested prioritizing simpler changes first. Ishan Nahata proposed an easier solution for custom plan merchants: instead of a custom view, they could skip the subscription flow entirely and hide all subscription-related content for these users. The discussion also included the need for education around credit models, focusing on wordings and design choices to ensure customers are comfortable with the idea without lying to them.
* Annual and Unlimited Plans for Unregistered Merchants Ishan Nahata brought up the concept of a 5,000 rupees annual plan or "forever unlimited" plan, mainly targeting unregistered (unreg) merchants who have sporadic transactions and might prefer a longer runway than monthly. This unlimited plan, particularly for UPI transactions, aims to extract the entire value upfront, and they will need to validate the pricing with marketing and finance. Ishan Nahata suggested limiting this to unreg merchants who satisfy a certain cap and considered a prepaid recharge model for credits where validity does not expire monthly.
* GMB Increase and Experiment Goals Ishan Nahata emphasized the importance of measuring the impact of plans on Gross Merchandise Value (GMB), specifically looking for an increase in GMB for users who take a plan, using a control versus test approach. The goal for December is to move the registered number by 2-3% (e.g., from 6% to 8%) through two design experiments, with a timeline to be provided soon. Aditya Mohan Gupta asked about the owners of the internal page and Ishan Nahata confirmed moving the internal page first until one or two experiments are figured out.
* Discovery Points and Cancellation State Ishan Nahata noted that 4,300 people have landed on the self-serve page and suggested taking down the current discovery points and re-launching them along with new efforts if the conversion rate from these points remains low. Ishan Nahata also mentioned that the team needs to finalize the proper cancellation initiated state, ensuring it communicates that the cancellation will occur in a set number of days.
* Smart Assist Vision and Alternative Solutions The team decided to focus on smart assist in January, allowing for a better vision of how it will function as a sales and contextual assistant, including potential voice modality and screen control features. Ishan Nahata discussed prototyping voice interactions using browser APIs and pre-recorded audio clips. Ishan Nahata also suggested evaluating existing agent solutions like Finn by Intercom or similar tools (e.g., those using 11 Labs) for trials instead of investing heavily in building their own smart assistant from scratch.
* Prioritization and Upcoming Meetings Gopi Bhatnagar asked about the plan for tomorrow, and Ishan Nahata outlined that the design team would close the cancellation bit in the first half, and then the three team members could brainstorm ideas in the second half. Ishan Nahata agreed with Gopi Bhatnagar to try to prioritize a low-effort UX fix, referencing a Stripe design where stating "X number of sales reps are available" boosted conversions. The team plans to have more calls later in the week to come up with the next set of ideas.


Suggested next steps
* Ishan Nahata will work with marketing and finances to validate the number for the expensive lifetime unlimited UPI plan.
* Ishan Nahata will close the reverse penny drop since Aisha is out.
* Ishan Nahata will evaluate existing solutions for smart assist to see if any can be experimented with instead of spending too much on the smart assistant.
* Ishan Nahata, Gopi Bhatnagar, and Aditya Mohan Gupta will sit together in the second half of tomorrow to brainstorm on ideas for design experiments.
