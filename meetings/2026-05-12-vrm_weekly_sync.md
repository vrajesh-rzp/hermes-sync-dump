Details
* VRM Project Overview: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar introduced the Virtual Relationship Manager (VRM) initiative, designed to manage merchant churn and increase incremental net revenue. The project addresses two types of merchants: those showing warning signals and those, comprising 42%, who churn without providing any explicit signals. By managing churn through improved merchant interaction, the team aims to reduce silent churn and stabilize merchant revenue.
* Churn Prediction Model and Indicators: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar explained that the current analytics-based churn prediction model utilizes a correlation metric based on long-term data, including active days and other parameters, though it currently operates at an accuracy rate of 40% to 45%. Indicators of potential churn include raised support tickets regarding issues like pricing or service breaks, and a shift in volume from payment gateways to payment links, which suggests the merchant may be exploring competitors. Seasonal patterns are also monitored, with filters applied based on historical Gross Merchandise Value data for merchants with over one year of tenure.
* Defining Churn and Degrowth Metrics: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar defined churn as a merchant stopping all transactions in a specific month after previously transacting. Degrowth is characterized by a 10% reduction in Gross Merchandise Value compared to the average of the last three months, utilizing a 90-day moving average window to account for seasonality. Currently, about 45% of total Gross Merchandise Value is affected by churn or degrowth, while the number of merchants who have churned stands at approximately 24% to 25% month-on-month. While 60% to 70% of degrown Gross Merchandise Value typically returns within 12 months, churned merchants have a lower probability of revival; therefore, the VRM project will focus primarily on churn prevention and growth management rather than revival.
* Monetization and Pilot Strategy: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar noted that there are currently no plans to charge for the VRM service due to limited bandwidth, though it may be reconsidered as a paid service if it gains sufficient traction. For the pilot launch, the team plans to target 10 merchants, with Apur serving as the pseudo account manager to monitor conversations and identify issues. The selection criteria for this cohort will involve balancing industry split, lifecycle stage—ranging from newly onboarded to scaled—and transaction frequency, with a requirement of 500 transactions to ensure statistical significance.
* Technical Infrastructure and Security: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar discussed two approaches for the development infrastructure: using current infrastructure, which is unlikely to receive security clearance, or adopting a more scalable, standard approach that requires more time. Given these technical challenges, the previously targeted launch timeline of Friday is expected to be delayed. Kadambary Diwan and Rishav Loomba are tasked with drafting a technical specification detailing these approaches for the team channel to facilitate further decision-making regarding security and development.
