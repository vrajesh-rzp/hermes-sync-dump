Summary
Ishan Nahata and Gopi Bhatnagar discussed closing UI designs, including icon concepts for "person valet" and agreeing on a cartoon aesthetic for e-commerce visuals, ensuring brand neutrality and focusing on top categories like dental care. Gopi Bhatnagar will prepare assets for the first and third versions of the design layout, with the second version pending review, while Ishan Nahata committed to dedicating one person to future design experiments.


Details
Notes Length: Standard
* UI Design and Iconography for Person Valet Ishan Nahata and Gopi Bhatnagar discussed closing out UI design elements, with Gopi Bhatnagar sharing they had worked on icon concepts for the "person valet". The conversation addressed the visual appearance of a button, which Gopi Bhatnagar noted looks like a button, and Ishan Nahata commented on the lighting change. They also confirmed that several items had been approved, and Ishan Nahata advised against working on the weekend due to someone being on leave, though Gopi Bhatnagar noted they might work since designs are closed.
* Illustration Style and E-commerce Visuals Gopi Bhatnagar showed an image generated with N suburb, which Ishan Nahata felt had an illustration style similar to Notion, and they decided to "tone it down". The focus shifted to concept visuals for e-commerce, specifically for the MCC one value, which Ishan Nahata suggested should include illustrations for top categories like dental care, using elements like a generic delivery person or objects like a box in the background to maintain consistency. They agreed that a cartoon aesthetic was preferable over a realistic one, which Gopi Bhatnagar felt looked too much like an advertisement.
* Composition of Visual Elements and Brand Neutrality Ishan Nahata emphasized the need to include elements of accounting alongside the person in the visuals, drawing parallels to older visuals where elements related to a brand were next to the person, like a yoga mat next to a Bliss Club person. Gopi Bhatnagar cautioned against highlighting any specific brand in the illustrations. Ishan Nahata suggested creating imagery that is generic but recognizable, like a person shopping on their phone, to make it relatable for education and e-commerce shopping.
* Plan Selection UI and Layout Hypotheses The discussion moved to the plan selection UI, where Ishan Nahata preferred a view that makes them want to click. They debated the presentation of three plans, with Gopi Bhatnagar explaining they had tilted the plans so it "didn't feel like I should hover" but noted that drop-offs increased if only one plan was shown. Ishan Nahata expressed concern that the plan's tilt made it look like a "tag," but they considered having the tilt occur on hover.
* Design Layout Challenges and A/B Testing The speakers discussed multiple layout variants for plan selection, including a table structure that Pingal was not keen on, which Gopi Bhatnagar noted they did not refine. Ishan Nahata outlined the hypotheses for different variants: simplifying information hierarchy and emphasizing "social proofing". A primary challenge identified was the disconnect between the expected monthly earnings slider and the recommended plan, noting that in the older design the slider was close to the card, but now this proximity is lost.
* Plan Card Design and Messaging They considered various UI configurations, including a bar graph to show savings relative to the free plan, which Ishan Nahata found promising but noted the visual should be closer to the card. Ishan Nahata recommended maintaining a single header line across the versions and using copy like "best for free". For the e-commerce visuals, the final decision was to have a person with some background elements conveying e-commerce, focusing on top categories, and not using stock photos.
* Education and Social Proofing in Variants Ishan Nahata stressed the importance of education over selling, particularly for understanding MDR, aiming for "white platforms not dark platforms". They agreed on testing variants focusing on social proofing and education, with one variant concentrating on social proofing and another on education. Gopi Bhatnagar inquired about the mode of communication for education, leading Ishan Nahata to clarify that it needs to explain how the plan works, including charges and payment structure.
* Next Steps and Design Handover Ishan Nahata mentioned that they would try to have one person dedicated to design experiments on the page going forward, suggesting they would relay data-driven decisions. Gopi Bhatnagar expressed concern about the hierarchy in a specific version, and Ishan Nahata directed them not to spend more time on it for now. Gopi Bhatnagar committed to preparing assets for the first and third versions, with the second version pending a look. They briefly discussed the "stackable cards" issue on the second version.


Suggested next steps
* Ishan Nahata will share a few more options next week based on social proofing for the education piece.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will tag Ishan Nahata on the Sunday one related to the hand-off.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will make the first and third versions hand off ready.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will prepare assets for the second version if Ishan Nahata wants to have a look.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will prepare only four stackable cards: education, healthcare, and the top two categories.
