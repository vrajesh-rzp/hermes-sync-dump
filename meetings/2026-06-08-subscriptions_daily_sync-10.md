Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Reviewing and Finalizing Plan Structures Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar and Gopi Bhatnagar discussed plan structures, with Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar noting that they would focus on the interaction and the current chapter. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar stated that the first plan structure, which emphasizes simplicity with callouts for levels 1, 2, and 3, is the one they will finalize as the "golden" option, pending UI and copy changes. They also discussed variations, including a focus on either lower Merchant Discount Rate (MDR) or free transactions.
* Transparency and Slider Functionality The speakers debated the level of transparency, with Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar advocating for making savings explicit to customers. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar acknowledged previous pushback on using a slider but suggested an alternative that functions similarly to a slider or input field for calculating savings. Ishan Nahata later suggested using the existing "blue bar and green bar" design language for usage limits, or an animation to show money flow and limit thresholds.
* Visualization and Communication Strategy Ishan Nahata proposed visualizations, such as a static graph showing different rates for the plans to help merchants understand the value proposition, acknowledging a concern about whether merchants are "mathematical enough". Ishan Nahata also suggested focusing the sales pitch on a single variable, such as free transactions, with the lower MDR as a secondary "add-on" benefit, perhaps visualized as a piggy bank filling up until a limit is reached. They decided to focus on closing one screen for the first preferred approach, while Ishan Nahata works on variations for the other approaches.
* Cancellation Flow and Merchant Win-Back The team discussed closing the cancellation flow, which Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata agreed was a long-pending item. Gopi Bhatnagar outlined the enhanced flow, including asking for the intent for cancellation, a confirmation transition state, and showing when benefits will expire. Ishan Nahata questioned the complexity of allowing self-served cancellation of a cancellation, while Gopi Bhatnagar highlighted the need to build a flow to allow customers to retain their plan within a time period, particularly addressing those who downgrade within 30 days.
* Post-Subscription Confidence and Copy Changes Ishan Nahata suggested that instead of focusing on "guilt tripping" merchants immediately after cancellation to win them back, they should prioritize reaffirming the benefits immediately post-purchase to prevent cancellations. They also addressed minor UI and copy concerns in the cancellation flow, such as standardizing the date format and ensuring the subtitle for the Enterprise plan does not appear as a call-to-action (CTA). Ishan Nahata also noted a bug where cancellation removes subscriptions altogether instead of defaulting to the light plan.
* Merchant Cancellation and Upgrade Data Ishan Nahata shared data regarding merchant activity, noting approximately 40 upgrades and 80 downgrades weekly, suggesting cancellation patterns should be the focus. They agreed to dedicate time to merchant calling this week to understand the cancellation patterns and validate design ideas.
* Review and Next Steps for Plan Screens Ishan Nahata detailed the next steps, prioritizing closing the single plan screen that has high confidence for handoff and mobile readiness. They confirmed the decision to present multiple plans as an industry standard for upgrading users. Ishan Nahata also discussed discarding "dark patterns" such as timers and other "cheap" approaches, as the overall brand vibe should be premium, and emphasized that the focus should be on simplicity and communicating savings explicitly.
* Pricing Display and Transparency Ishan Nahata addressed displaying yearly vs. monthly pricing, suggesting calling out savings explicitly for the yearly plan (e.g., 40% lesser pricing) and providing two buttons for monthly versus yearly commitment. They also emphasized that the free plan must not be hidden, even while communicating that it is not the best deal. Finally, they briefly discussed the need for finance approval on potential UPI or specific product-focused plan variations, and the continued debate around the subscription model's efficacy.


Suggested next steps
* Ishan Nahata will spend time on figuring out one variant of each approach for the two screens and will shut down the single plan variant.
* Ishan Nahata will check the date format to ensure it is standardized everywhere.
