Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar proposed shifting to a two-week sprint cycle, which Nikhil Uday Khadilkar agreed would help with priority maintenance, and they discussed the capability track's expected completion by early next week to allow marketing experiments. The participants reviewed the "subscription capability" track, which aims to convert traffic and identify high-quality leads, with Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar acknowledging the ambitious goal of acquiring phone numbers for direct sales contact, while Nikhil Uday Khadilkar cited bandwidth and bug-related issues as current challenges. The complexity of the Smart Assist feature was noted by Nikhil Uday Khadilkar, who explained that the two original developers have left, and the system's reliance on LLM and intricate logic makes handoff difficult for the new person, Suas, leading Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar to suggest involving Suas or Jasper given their familiarity with the codebase. Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Sprint Structure and Capability Track Timeline Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar proposed moving to a two-week sprint cycle. Nikhil Uday Khadilkar agreed that this would help ensure better priority maintenance but noted it was previously only an internal discussion. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar noted that the capability track, which includes banners and plotline gamification, is expected to be completed by Monday or Tuesday of the next week, allowing the marketing team to start experiments without requiring further engineering effort.
* Subscription Track and High-Quality Leads The discussion moved to the "subscription capability" track, focused on converting traffic to sign-ups and identifying high-quality leads for the sales team. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar described ambitions to identify phone numbers of high-quality website leads for direct contact and conversion, acknowledging that this is an ambitious approach. Nikhil Uday Khadilkar noted that the current challenge is bandwidth, citing pressure to complete some tech items next week and highlighting an issue where team members must work on bugs instead of critical priorities.
* Smart Assist and Technical Complexity The conversation touched on issues with the Smart Assist feature, specifically that the two people who worked on it have left the team, and much of the logic is complex and relies on LLM and intricate flows. Nikhil Uday Khadilkar mentioned that a new person, Suas, has limited context, making it difficult to hand off tasks, and that the Smart Assist system is very complex despite appearing simple because users can type anything into it and it utilizes a knowledge base. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar suggested involving Suas or Jasper since they might be familiar with the Smart Assist codebase.
* Subscription Track Modules and Merchant Education Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar discussed the need to scope out the subscription track, which involves defining the exact modules to build and their complexity. They mentioned that a core insight is that merchants are taking the free plan because they do not understand how credits work, leading to a proposal for a small UI change to educate merchants. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar also set a goal for the next sprint to allocate no more than 20% of effort to reliability and 80% to feature development for the subscription track.
