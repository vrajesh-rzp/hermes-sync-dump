Details
* Project Management and AI Focus: The meeting started with the intent to review all projects and assign ownership, noting that one participant will be more involved in AI projects, shifting the burden of other projects onto others. It was decided that for many projects, the function of the PM will be to guide the task through graduation and budget allocation. The discussion began by listing engineers and people responsible for running the projects day-to-day (00:02:18).
* Website Traffic Analysis and Component Development: The team discussed the need for clarity regarding website traffic, specifically the percentage of new traffic, repeat traffic, and current merchants. It was noted that while some components like the SSO login were already created, they require some text adjustments because of a redirection to the USA. For website changes, it was agreed that the framework necessitates continued adjustments as most changes are ongoing (00:02:18).
* Sales Agent and AI Platform Use Cases: The sales agent role and its supporting engineering lead were identified for website tasks, including anything involving downloads. The discussion confirmed that all identified tasks, such as inbound/outbound processing, web scraping, and qualification, are different use cases for the AI platform. The engineering required for the AI component building, including the AI calling platform, will necessitate additional support (00:06:18).
* Competition Detection and Funnel Analysis: The team confirmed the necessity of detecting if users in the sign-up funnel but who have not completed the process have joined a competitor. While a detection system is in place, its coverage is low, requiring efforts to replicate flows and find solutions for websites without a visible checkout feature (00:09:03). The goal for competition detection is to win over the detected leads, making the detection and follow-up part of the overall strategy (00:11:54).
* Identifying and Prioritizing High-Quality Leads (HQLs): A key discussion point was prioritizing leads that are High-Quality Leads (HQLs) and who are already using a competitor. The process of finding these leads involves using tools like Build with API to get website lists and then cross-referencing with other data sources like Google and ad signals, which should be done monthly to refresh traffic data (00:13:30). The task of building this system will need to be assigned, possibly to the existing HQ team, and involves modeling (00:19:17).
* Sales Process Replication and AI Error Qualification: The team discussed defining a "system of attack" to reach out to high-priority leads using five different channels, replicating a sales process via LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and email. They also discussed implementing AI-based error qualification, aiming for 100% coverage, which will utilize the calling platform for a new use case. The overall website effort is intended to solve the contact visibility issue in the dashboard (00:19:17).
* Gamification and Communication Synchronization: A discussion on gamification involved implementing a banner, with the core sentiment focused on productivity gamification. It was decided that communications across all platforms must be synchronized, ensuring follow-up communications are sent if the initial contact is unsuccessful. The team also acknowledged a timeline uncertainty regarding a project related to increased metrics and agreed to a weekly review (00:22:04).
* Multi-PG Merchant Detection and Win Strategy: The team discussed the detection of multi-payment gateway (Multi-PG) merchants, defining it as a strategy to determine "wallet share" in payment ecosystem providers (EP) (00:24:09). The focus is not just on detection but on winning the business, using the detection as an input for the win strategy. The detection process needs to achieve a high accuracy, and the subsequent data should be pushed to the Super system for outbound calling (00:26:24).
* Gamification Implementation and Subscription Work: The engineering lead for gamification is K, and the team will work with the communications design team to implement constructs (00:26:24). The team reviewed the existing work on gamification and confirmed the need to create a list of experiments for the quarter and year. For active work on the subscription part, one person was suggested to work with an existing team member due to the existing member's familiarity with the subscription system (00:28:29).
* Analytics and Control Group Biasing: A significant issue was identified in the analytics related to control group biasing in A/B testing. Specifically, a gap exists between qualifying conditions and variant assignments, causing non-qualifying merchants and partners to be incorrectly grouped as part of the control group (00:31:16). This analytics fix is currently being worked on to resolve the clubbing of non-payment gateway (non-PG) India merchants with others (00:33:53).
* Bug Fixes and Independent Project Management: The requirement for fixing all bugs was affirmed, and the PM for a running project (likely subscription/gamification) was directed to run it independently with their assigned engineer and designer group. The focus is on ensuring the system can be operated by the team without constant external support (00:33:53).
* Rewards and Waitlist Advertising: The team discussed the implementation of credits and rewards at the method level, potentially using them for B2B advertisements through scratch cards, similar to how Zepto operates (00:37:00). This approach aims to create a positive user experience, focusing on the feeling of happiness upon scratching the reward card (00:40:31).
* Merchant Recognition and Platform Integration: The discussion moved to a program of merchant recognition, which includes a KYC verification badge and a "trusted" badge for the top level (00:40:31). These badges could be integrated as scratch rewards and encouraged for use on merchant websites. The idea of a new commerce creation process, possibly involving inspection, was brought up (00:43:47).
* Go-to-Market (GTM) Strategy and B2B/B2C Definitions: Concerns were raised about the current Go-to-Market work, which was considered ineffective, particularly because the company operates in a hybrid space between B2B and B2C due to its small business focus. The need to refine the GTM strategy specifically for their company size and structure was emphasized (00:47:03).
* Merchant Health Report and Churn Model Accuracy: The discussion covered the development of a merchant health report (a 360-degree view) that will reside with a specific participant. This involves leveraging the existing churn model, which was not initially configured for smaller websites. The accuracy of the churn detection model, which is currently at 52% when analyzing merchants detected by the model versus those generated by a VA, was noted as not being "bad" (00:53:51).
* Workflow Triggers and Connector Work: The team discussed the importance of the calling platform's capabilities, which need to be built to support a UK calling agent triggered by a specific workflow injection (00:48:58). The final topic concerned the significant work required on connectors, which should enable the ability to take action based on people who leave a trace in the system (00:56:34).
* Detecting Silent Merchants and Proactive Engagement: A hypothesis was discussed regarding merchant retention, noting that painful right to removal is costly. Two methods for detecting silent merchants include proactive engagement and tracking decreasing traffic volume. The avenue to discuss issues, provided by VM, is one hypothesis for addressing this (00:58:45).
* Assignments and Metric Cadence: The discussion involved assigning certain tasks, including a 20% removal of Google ads and competition detection. The charter owner for each initiative will establish a regular cadence, such as weekly reporting, with defined output metrics like project progress. Weekly updates are expected to be shared by the assigned parties (01:00:50).
* Staff Meetings and Q1 Deliverables: The team needs to establish a regular meeting structure, such as a weekly market update for the team system and big staff or monthly staff meetings. The immediate focus is identifying projects most likely to deliver results in Q1, Q2, and Q3, while acknowledging the challenge of staffing changes where key people might be swapped for various reasons (01:03:14).
* Quality Budgets and Resource Allocation: A concern was raised that no quality budgets are currently accounted for, suggesting that at least 20-25% of engineering bandwidth should be dedicated to quality each quarter. The discussion also addressed the need to assign specific people to continuous, non-project related tasks (01:10:20).
* Competition Detection and Platform Building: Two open questions were identified: the bifurcated experience and outbound hunting/scripting (01:10:20). The competition detection involves scraping merchant information to determine if they are engaging with competitors after signing up with the company, which is considered part of the overall platform building. This requires platform capabilities to respond to such situations reactively (01:14:36).
* Independent Evaluation and Wrong Responses: Independent evaluations were conducted with 10 groups of merchants using the same queries and language. The evaluations indicated that some wrong responses were being given, and one party is not willing to sign off on the results (01:19:06).
* Measuring Chat Quality and Sentiment Analysis: There is a need to discuss core requirements and observability, which currently includes dashboards measuring latency. Chat quality is currently measured using sentiment analysis that is integrated into the system, and this scoring is based on the completeness of the chat quality (01:21:16).
* Proactive vs. Reactive Outreach: The team decided to move forward with both proactive and reactive outreach methods. The most immediate value is expected to come from reactive measures, as proactive outreach is already being handled through other channels like email. The team plans to continue sending email communications but will also begin sending communications via WhatsApp to improve engagement and monitor open rates (01:24:38).
* Deep Thinking and Requirement Documentation: There is a need for deep thinking about how the proposed solutions will convert to the desired output and why they matter. The discussion concluded with an acknowledgment that the requirements are understood and that deep thought, documentation, and consolidation of analytics functions are needed (01:27:34).
