Summary
Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata discussed various design elements, including platform fee visualization, aesthetic refinements, and accessibility concerns, such as the gold color not meeting Double A standards, leading Ishan Nahata to suggest making the gold lighter or the text darker. Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Design Exploration and Review of Platform Fee Visualization Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata discussed the visualization of platform fee variations and free monthly limits. Ishan Nahata emphasized the need to clearly call out that the amount is free and highlighted the 30x monthly limit and 28% lower platform fee. They also reviewed the aesthetic of the e-commerce design, noting that it does not look center-aligned and that the static statement about the file is distracting.
* Visual Design and Accessibility Feedback The participants reviewed color choices, with Ishan Nahata suggesting using darker shades and potentially a color gradient in the background, though they preferred a lighter background for solving a current issue. Gopi Bhatnagar raised concerns about gold's accessibility, noting it doesn't meet Double A standards, which prompted Ishan Nahata to suggest making the gold lighter or the text darker to improve accessibility. They agreed that the current exploration with the side-by-side design is nice and that they would play around with fills.
* Mobile Strategy and Prioritization Ishan Nahata stated that mobile is not a current focus for major changes, indicating that mobile hygiene fixes would only reduce tickets rather than providing a boost, based on the hypothesis and feedback that people are clicking but needing exploration. The speakers agreed that they would check mobile numbers and traffic percentages, especially since the mobile flow is at the end of the onboarding process, which is linked to drop-offs. The prioritization was set: first, finalize the first version for handoff, then work on the second and third versions, and finally, address mobile cleanup.
* Three Versions of Card Visualization and Social Validation The discussion revolved around three versions of card visualizations, with a key focus on incorporating social validation. Ishan Nahata noted that they should evaluate on the current concept, but acknowledged that Sony's feedback might necessitate a complete change to try equal-sized cards. Gopi Bhatnagar introduced button copy versions such as "save more with plus" and "claim savings with plus," and suggested adding a percentage for savings. Ishan Nahata also suggested closing the third version, which focuses on social validation, and incorporating a graph and table to make it feel more like a table with a heading.
* Design Elements and Layout Refinements Ishan Nahata recommended using a 30x monthly limit or similar language instead of "display" copy, which they noted should be reserved for websites. Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata aligned on a three-column layout for better clarity and alignment, with the first column left-aligned, the second column (for numbers) right-aligned, and the columns having equal width to manage text expansion without affecting the UI. Ishan Nahata also provided input on making the category name stand out, suggesting using a different color, making it bolder, or adding an icon or emoji as an asset for stronger visual association.
* Design Aesthetics and Color Choices The speakers reviewed color choices, with Ishan Nahata noting that the blue felt fine but reminded them of "Razorpay". They agreed to try more colors since the darker ones were not working and the current blue with gold looked "sandy". Ishan Nahata also emphasized that the central Call to Action (CTA) needed to be more "powerful" compared to side CTAs. Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata discussed not adding a fill to the card component but rather using an image and background image for the asset, ensuring it looks fine on both white and dark cards.
* Interaction and Card Design for Horizontal Stack Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata discussed the behavior of the third card, focusing on selectable states and transitions, especially since there is no slider. Ishan Nahata emphasized the need for a primary way of interaction, such as hovering, to select or open the card. They discussed horizontal stacking as a way to visually connect effects in the card more closely to the elements, making it easier for the user to click the CTA without cognitive effort.
* Table Aesthetic and Bulletin Post Strategy Ishan Nahata confirmed they would work on creating an "aesthetic table" version, which was a direction they moved toward due to the problem of too much repeating text in the original versions. Gopi Bhatnagar inquired about working on a bulletin post, and they decided to prepare a version of the text to post later that day or early the next morning, ideally together with Ishan Nahata. They concluded the meeting with plans for Gopi Bhatnagar to update the handoff file and for them to push one of the versions to go live soon.


Suggested next steps
* Gopi Bhatnagar will pull the button options from the handoff file and add the versions to the file.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will work on the third version of the cards to explore social validation, clean it up, add a graph, and make it feel more like a table with a heading.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will ensure that the width of the cards in the design is the same and give thought to the interaction for the third card, which has selectable states and transition, including checking the idea of horizontally stacked cards.
* Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata will work on, review, and share the first version of the text for the pro bulletin post today or early tomorrow.
