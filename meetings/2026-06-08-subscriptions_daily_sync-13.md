﻿📝 Notes

Subscriptions Daily Sync

Attachments Subscriptions Daily Sync 


Summary
Ishan Nahata and Gopi Bhatnagar discussed design feedback, with Ishan Nahata noting an icon was missing "sparkle," and they decided to remove overwhelming background elements and change symmetrical 3D designs that did not fit the current design language. Ishan Nahata shared that November subscription revenue was eight lakhs, mainly from lump payments, and detailed feedback from Pingal regarding a calculator visual where the minimal plan value delta was a concern, prompting suggestions for higher deltas, visual checkpoints, and interactive prototyping. They decided to incorporate social validation assets based on merchant categories and review copy variations, addressing the problematic use of "free" in plan descriptions and aiming to have the primary design options and cancellation flow "hand off ready" by tomorrow noon.


Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Setting up the Meeting and Design Feedback Ishan Nahata and Gopi Bhatnagar opened the meeting, initially discussing camera setup and the visual presence of background elements, deciding that removing some visual elements was necessary as it felt "too much" and "too overwhelming". Ishan Nahata provided feedback on an icon, noting that it was missing a "sparkle" and felt more like a design element. They also discussed symmetrical and 3D design elements, which Ishan Nahata felt did not fit the current design language and would need to change.
* Subscription Revenue and Cohort Analysis Ishan Nahata shared that they had made eight lakhs in November from subscription bundles, compared to five lakhs from full subscription bundles made by marketing. This eight lakhs figure only includes lump payments made directly to that account in November and excludes people who took annual plans in October, as well as money made from people breaching their spending limits. Ishan Nahata estimated that with 800 people on paid plans, the average payment was around $1,000.
* Team Structure and Time Off Ishan Nahata mentioned the team's structure, which includes two designers and an 11-member engineering team. They also discussed upcoming time off, with Ishan Nahata planning a vacation to Goa from the 19th to the 22nd and wanting time off during the festive season. Gopi Bhatnagar also mentioned wanting some time off.
* Feedback on the Calculator/Visual and Design Delta Ishan Nahata detailed feedback from an individual, Pingal, who was initially sold on the idea of a calculator visual but became against it after seeing the math, as the delta in value between plans was minimal. Ishan Nahata suggested exploring a higher delta in the interactive visual to encourage movement between plans. However, Ishan Nahata acknowledged that while they can skew the visual, the base values cannot be skewed.
* Addressing Math and Checkpoints for Plans The discussion shifted to the math supporting the plan comparison, noting that the graph was not updating correctly when values were being tracked. Ishan Nahata suggested creating "checkpoints" in the visual thinking at key thresholds, such as 30,000 and 150,000, which represent the best plans. Ishan Nahata proposed highlighting these points to guide the user's thinking and to allow locking on to 5,000 increments.
* Visualizing Delta and Interactive Prototyping Ishan Nahata reviewed a Google Sheet showing the financial delta between plans, explaining that the color coding indicated that the "redder it is, the better" it is for the user to move to the next plan. They noted that the presentation failed previously because it went up to 115k, which made the perceived value difference smaller, but clarified that 125k shows a clearer benefit. Ishan Nahata suggested creating an interactive prototype with the actual numbers.
* Social Validation Assets and Copy Changes Ishan Nahata discussed incorporating social validation into the design, suggesting custom assets based on top merchant categories like "fashion" to show that "XYZ merchants signed up today". Gopi Bhatnagar confirmed that the individual Pingal is keen on incorporating these elements. They also discussed copy feedback, noting that calling the plan "free" was problematic, especially when trying to highlight the difference between plans.
* Term and CTA Copy Decisions Ishan Nahata stated that the term "monthly free limit" was acceptable but highlighting "free" in all plans defeats the purpose. They discussed keeping "monthly free limit" regular or saying "free until". Ishan Nahata agreed to test out more copy variations, including CTA copy, as these experiments require minimum effort and potentially offer high ROI.
* TNC Page Content and Design Ishan Nahata raised concerns about the Terms and Conditions (TNC) page, stating that the content and writing style were poor and looked like "someone made notes," even though Gopi Bhatnagar created it. Ishan Nahata mentioned involving a technical writer and using GPT to help with the copy. They agreed that this was a content issue rather than a design issue.
* Interactive Chart Simulation and Dark Patterns Ishan Nahata outlined a plan for the interactive chart, where the slider will snap onto 500 increments and onto the key thresholds of 30k and 150k. They discussed using a "merchant backwards dark pattern," where people are encouraged to select a better plan based on optimistic earnings, which was an insight from a previous marketing exercise.
* Choosing Design Variants and Hand-off Readiness Ishan Nahata expressed inclination towards one design version over the chart variant due to its focus on one plan and less overwhelming information. They aimed to close the design of both primary options and get them "hand off ready" by tomorrow noon. Ishan Nahata emphasized the importance of checking contrast and toning down the UI. Gopi Bhatnagar confirmed they would make the cancellation flow "hand off ready" as well, since it is pending review.
* Mobile Design Approach and Traffic The discussion touched on the mobile version, with Gopi Bhatnagar suggesting a horizontal layout. Ishan Nahata suggested checking mobile traffic and conversion to determine if an experiment should be run on mobile, otherwise, they may keep the mobile version the same. They noted that high mobile traffic and bad conversion might be due to accidental clicks on the paid plan.
* Dark Patterns and Ethical Design Ishan Nahata sought non-dark patterns that would give the same results as the current strategies. They acknowledged the current strategy (offering real savings on MT) is not as "dark" as some other ideas, such as fake sold-out plans. Ishan Nahata emphasized the need to define the line between acceptable and unacceptable dark patterns.
* Future Experiments and Social Proofing They decided to run experiments on static design variants and asset exploration, requiring limited engineering effort. Ideas for future experiments include adding FAQs or something like "What is Pricing" on click. They also planned to explore social proofing, either showing a count of people who recently signed up or using MCC-based social proofing. Ishan Nahata also suggested exploring minor changes like button copy variations for experiments.
* Copy Strategy and Savings Ishan Nahata raised a question about whether two copies within one screen should be very different, acknowledging that the company profits regardless of which option a customer chooses. Gopi Bhatnagar agreed that the copies should be different, but suggested they would "try something else out on the savings bit".
* Testimonials Gopi Bhatnagar asked for clarification on the two components of the testimonials, noting that "MCC is one labor". Ishan Nahata explained that the second testimonial component is showing the exact count of merchants, like "XYZ merchants took this offer" recently, possibly within the last three months, to provide an actual number.


Suggested next steps
* Ishan Nahata will spend time today to test out the demo recordings.
* Ishan Nahata will sit with marketing tomorrow to get their views on the experience of all the recommended banners.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will get both versions of the design hand off ready by noon tomorrow, excluding button copy variations.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will make the cancellation flow design hand off ready to close it.
* Ishan Nahata will spend time on the chart and all savings for the design options, and see if they can spend time on the asset concept wise.
* Gopi Bhatnagar will try something else out on the savings bit.
