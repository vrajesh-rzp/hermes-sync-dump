Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Subscription Plan Clarification and Strategy The meeting began with Ishan Nahata seeking clarity on finalizing subscription plans and Pingal Kakati checking on the status, as Ishan Nahata hadn't connected with Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar yet. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar explained that they had been in meetings, and they discussed the listed five plans, noting that some were similar, with the difference being the explicit visibility of the 10K GMBB limit. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar proposed that an annual plan and a monthly plan, referred to as Plan A and Plan B, could constitute one experiment, where the monthly plan would be a $4.99 all-inclusive paid plan up to 10 lakhs.
* Plan Differentiation and Packaging Pingal Kakati questioned if all options would be presented as a single subscription, to which Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar clarified that they would not be all presented together. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar suggested two main plan structures: an upfront annual payment of $49.99 or a monthly payment of $4.99 for the same benefits, both up to 10 lakhs, while the UPI plan could be a separate experiment with a $2.99 fee for free UPI transactions. Ishan Nahata sought the rationale for the explicit inclusion of certain variants, such as the difference between UPIC and UPIE, and they agreed that A, D, and E were the three distinctly different plans.
* Annual Plan Pricing and Merchant Risk Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar confirmed that each plan would also have a monthly and an annual version, with the annual plan typically offering two months free. The group raised concerns about the monthly plan's pricing, where $4.99 was considered too cheap for 10 lakhs worth of free credits, especially compared to the current $4.99 for up to 30,000. Pingal Kakati and Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar expressed worry about high-transacting users taking the subscription while low-transacting users do not, which could negatively impact profitability, although they believed they could manage this risk.
* Plan Display and User Behavior Ishan Nahata proposed rough versions of the plan display, focusing on a zero-fee plan, a monthly plan, and a UPI plan, potentially using a prepaid/postpaid model. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar questioned why the annual plan was not the default, and Ishan Nahata argued that shorter subscriptions like 3 months are more common, and unread merchants might not survive for a whole year. Pingal Kakati worried that defaulting to an annual plan might deter users, leading them to abandon the sign-up process when they see the cost, even though Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar thought the math only occurs at the checkout.
* Simplifying the Plan Structure Gopi Bhatnagar asked for clarification on whether the plans were only monthly and annual. Pingal Kakati argued that they should simplify the offering to essentially two plans: an all-methods plan and a UPI-only plan, as anything else adds unnecessary complication. Ishan Nahata agreed that focusing on UPI only could be very effective, as most merchants start as UPI-only users. Pingal Kakati scheduled a follow-up discussion with Gopi Bhatnagar for the next day to work on the plan design.
* Miscellaneous Topics and Wrap-up The discussion briefly touched upon unrelated matters, including reviewing data on registered and unregistered users, identifying high-value merchants, and an impromptu thought about recruiting startups. The participants also made plans for a lunch and board games session, and they wished each other a Happy New Year before concluding the meeting.


Suggested next steps
