Details
* Lead Status Analysis: Apoorv S Chaturvedi and Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar reviewed a dataset containing 528 connected leads to differentiate between controllable and non-controllable categories. The analysis identified 2,441 controllable leads and 2,847 non-controllable leads, with the latter group consisting of accounts involving business shutdowns or holds that require no further action from the team.
* Categorizing Non-Controllable Leads: Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar noted that the non-controllable category may contain false positives, as some instances of perceived degrowth are linked to merchant-side business issues rather than Razor Pay actions. They discussed the necessity of potentially refining the classification to separate these merchant issues from actual Razor Pay-driven degrowth to ensure more accurate data analysis.
* Standard Operating Procedures for Ticket Resolution: The participants discussed the 30% to 40% of non-controllable cases that originate from customer tickets or preferences. Apoorv S Chaturvedi explained that ticket resolution is currently managed on a case-by-case basis, with issues routed to specific teams such as pricing, international payments, or integration departments. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar proposed that they formalize these processes to develop a Virtual Relationship Manager capable of automating these escalation workflows.
* Flowchart Documentation: To support the creation of the Virtual Relationship Manager, Apoorv S Chaturvedi agreed to draft a comprehensive flowchart detailing current human intervention processes for handling these issues. Apoorv S Chaturvedi plans to finalize this document, verify it with relevant team members, and share it with Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar by the following day.
* Top 750 Account Analysis: Apoorv S Chaturvedi and Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar examined data regarding 13,000 degrowth and churn accounts and the performance of top 750 account managers. They noted that the company successfully arrested a 20% drop in Gross Merchandise Value through focused issue resolution and merchant breakdowns. Furthermore, they reviewed a competition win-back initiative involving 16 merchants where the company regained 100% of the wallet share, resulting in a 5.5 direct Gross Merchandise Value impact.
* AI Calling Platform Implementation: Regarding the AI calling platform, Apoorv S Chaturvedi reported that the team is currently reaching 13,000 out of a 77,000-account target. Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar suggested that if results prove positive, the platform should be formalized, noting that reaching the remaining accounts could potentially lead to an additional 5% in savings. The participants acknowledged that these figures, representing data as of mid-April, provide a reliable directional split for their strategy.
