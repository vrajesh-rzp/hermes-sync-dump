Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Adding Members and Plotline Constructs Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar provided instructions for adding members to Plotline, noting that new users should be granted admin rights for the current purposes, but other roles like publisher or editor are available for future junior staff or designers. They explained that Plotline consists of various constructs, with a current focus on "games," specifically "luck games" such as spin the wheel, scratch cards, and slot machines. They also covered "milestones," which share a similar construct and include four stages, starting with a setup location that must use the "PG home" page and the "Plotline GMBB widget" element.
* Milestone Game Configuration and Content Iyengar detailed the configuration of milestone games, emphasizing that the "content" section is crucial for base steps, step one, step two, and step three, each represented by a renderable image on the dashboard, ideally created by design. They explained that each step includes a click action, which can be set to "nothing happens," "open a URL" (supporting deep links and UTM parameters), "copy some text," or frequently, "end flow" to chain multiple constructs together, such as launching a quiz after a floater is clicked. The linking mechanism involves setting a trigger for the subsequent step to be "on the end flow of this one".
* Setting Step Conditions with Backend Events Vrajesh Jaidev Iyengar explained how to define the qualifying conditions for a merchant to move into a new milestone step, often based on backend events related to Gross Merchandise Buy Back (GMBB) or transaction volume. They specified using the "transaction update multiod" backend event, which is piped every six hours, and applying properties to filter by total GMBB over various periods, such as the last 7 days or current month. An example was provided where step one requires a total GMBB of at least 5,000, and step two requires at least 10,000, using available comparators. Step three, beyond GMBB, can also incorporate a transaction counter, such as requiring at least 20,000 transactions.
* Finalizing Milestone Setup and Scheduling Iyengar concluded by discussing image editing for each step, which supports uploads from a device, maintaining the aspect ratio, and experimental AI-generated images, as well as GIF format for interactivity. They also explained setting up the "Audience" using segments, with a test segment used as an example, before proceeding to "save and proceed". Finally, they addressed "Scheduling," noting that campaigns can be set to go live at a specific date or immediately upon publishing, and the campaign can end either by manually pausing it or at a specified date and time, suggesting to allow users to view the completed reward card.
