Details
Notes Length: Standard
* Input and Slider Design for Subscription Amount Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata discussed approaches for the text input of the subscription amount, focusing on keeping the amount prominent. Ishan Nahata expressed concern that having the number on the side felt repetitive and suggested that exact number accuracy is not critical, favoring the ease of dragging the slider over typing the number. They explored an option to add more break points on the slider to guide the user, such as multiples of 10,000, without displaying the full plan details immediately.
* Plan Recommendations and Value Proposition The team considered making the plan recommendation more personalized, suggesting messaging like "based on your monthly earnings" to replace the current generic copy. Ishan Nahata pointed out that the value proposition of savings, such as "save up to 750 per year with plus plan," felt repetitive since savings were already shown elsewhere, and Gopi Bhatnagar agreed that the amount being displayed twice meant one could be removed for layout improvement. Ishan Nahata also highlighted a missing context that a user would need to know, noting that the reason for selecting a plan was unclear, which Pingal had previously mentioned.
* Design of Plan Cards and Visual Focus Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata reviewed card designs, with Gopi Bhatnagar noting that a previous design with a focus on the text input distracted from the plan cards and Call to Action (CTA). Ishan Nahata preferred a design that minimized the focus on the input after initial interaction. Ishan Nahata expressed a strong preference for more distinct card designs, as the current version felt too much like a section rather than individual plans, referencing WhatsApp and Slack as examples of less defined sections.
* Design System Compliance and Card Padding Gopi Bhatnagar noted an issue where changing the card corner radius to 8 pixels raised a non-compliant issue with the design system, reverting it back to 4 pixels to avoid issues with the braid score. Ishan Nahata and Gopi Bhatnagar also discussed minimal padding in one of the card versions, with Ishan Nahata mentioning that there was barely any padding between the edge and the cards, which was exacerbated by the white color.
* AI Literacy and Checklist Integration Gopi Bhatnagar asked about progress on AI literacy, and Ishan Nahata mentioned that three things were being worked on, including an AI checklist to ensure basic coverage for medium and larger projects. The checklist would cover aspects like user insights, competitor analysis, and whether AI was used for explanations or brainstorming alternative approaches, with both agreeing that prototyping with AI is essential.
* Spot and Focus Chatbot Discussion Satyam Mishra joined to discuss a chatbot on the razor.com non-frame page, explaining that it is designed to assist users with robust payment gateway solutions by pulling information from a sales agent knowledge base or commonly asked questions. Ishan Nahata noted that the current implementation was a native integration without specific APIs and that the latency seemed better than the 'smart assist' feature.
* Subscription Experiment Planning Gopi Bhatnagar and Ishan Nahata confirmed that the work on the subscription plan designs (Experiment 1 and 2) was largely complete, with only minor feedback remaining and the need to add breakpoints to the slider since it is not a blade component. Ishan Nahata shared data suggesting that Plan E, which has a higher charge, works well in the UPI category and that the team needs to figure out how to address a 'loss proposition' for users with over 10K GMV, potentially by not showing it to them. They discussed running a third experiment focused on the annual plan in isolation, considering Plan D and Plan E designs, and potentially setting the default to the annual plan.
* In-House Agent Development for Chatbot Ishan Nahata reported issues with voice interaction on the Spot and Focus tool, suggesting that they should build the whole agentic panel in-house to avoid potential breakage at larger scale. They discussed defining prompts in a config file and building a similar agent that can be synced with the marketing team to improve capabilities. Ishan Nahata also mentioned that they should evaluate other partners like 11 labs for the conversational interface.
* Voice and Video Integration for Smart Assist UI Ishan Nahata discussed the new Smart Assist UI, which they had pitched as a full-screen experience rather than a search bar. They reviewed a video-optimized design featuring a conversational agent named Manish, which Gopi Bhatnagar found less effective than the conversational format. Ishan Nahata stated that they should track engagement to assess if the visual engagement factor of video is worth the integration effort, deciding to first go live with audio only.
* WhatsApp Integration for Chatbot Ishan Nahata confirmed the context of seamless WhatsApp integration for the chatbot, aiming to capture the user's mobile number, which would require setting up a WhatsApp bot and implementing a constant reference ID to maintain conversation context across platforms. Satyam Mishra suggested renaming the contact to "Razor Pay Bot" instead of "WhatsApp". The team decided to focus on building the agent and aim for a demo with the team the following Friday, rather than aiming for handoff readiness by Monday.


Suggested next steps
